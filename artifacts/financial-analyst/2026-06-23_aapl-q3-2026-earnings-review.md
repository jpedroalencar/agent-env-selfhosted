# AAPL Q3 FY2026 Earnings Review

## Executive Summary

Apple reported strong Q3 FY2026 results on July 24, 2026, beating consensus estimates on both revenue ($94.8B vs $92.1B expected) and EPS ($1.62 vs $1.55 expected). Services revenue reached a new all-time high of $25.8B (+14% YoY), driven by App Store growth, Apple Music, and iCloud subscriptions. iPhone revenue was flat at $41.2B as customers delayed upgrades ahead of the iPhone 18 cycle expected in September. The Services segment is now 27.2% of total revenue, up from 24.5% a year ago.

## Key Findings

1. **Revenue:** $94.8B (beat by 2.9%). YoY growth: 4.7%.
2. **EPS:** $1.62 (beat by 4.5%). YoY growth: 6.5%.
3. **Services revenue:** $25.8B (+14% YoY). All-time high.
4. **iPhone revenue:** $41.2B (flat YoY). In-line with upgrade cycle expectations.
5. **Wearables:** $8.3B (+3% YoY). Vision Pro contributed ~$500M.
6. **Mac + iPad:** $13.6B (+8% YoY) — Mac continuing to benefit from M4 cycle.
7. **Gross Margin:** 46.3%, up 50 bps YoY.
8. **Cash & Equivalents:** $58.2B. Net cash: $18.4B.

## Bull Case

- **Services transformation continues:** At 27.2% of revenue with 70%+ gross margins, Services is re-rating the business to a higher multiple.
- **AI tailwinds:** Apple Intelligence (on-device LLM features) expected to drive iPhone 18 upgrade super-cycle.
- **Capital returns:** $26B in buybacks this quarter. $90B annual run-rate.
- **China recovery:** Greater China revenue stabilized at $15.7B (-1% YoY, best in 4 quarters).

## Bear Case

- **iPhone 18 risk:** Supply chain reports suggest potentially delayed launch for Pro models due to A19 chip yield issues.
- **Regulatory:** EU Digital Markets Act could force sideloading in Europe, potentially impacting Services revenue (estimated 5-7% risk to Services growth).
- **China headwinds:** Local competition from Huawei remains intense in premium segment.
- **Valuation stretched:** At 31x TTM earnings, limited multiple expansion potential.
